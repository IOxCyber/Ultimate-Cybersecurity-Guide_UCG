1. Threat Modeling: [Click](https://www.threatmodelingmanifesto.org/)
- Creates a model of all the potential threats to your application & mitigate them.

2. Attacks:
- Needs 3 things ie. Target(DB), Threat Vector(Secret Ways to sneak), Threat Actor (Malicious User)

3. Who should be involved:
- System Architect, Developer, Tester, Security Professional.

4. When to perform:
- Should be done in `Design` phase of SDLC.
- SDLC(Requirement gathering, design, Development, testing, deployment  Maintenance)

5. Threat Types:
- Spoofing (Authenticity)
- Tampering (Integrity)
- Repudiation (Non-Repudiability)
- Information Disclosure (Confidentiality)
- DoS (Availablity)
- Elevation of Privilege (Authz)

6. Response & Mitigation:
- Mitigate (Reduce the likelihood)
- Eliminate (Remove the feature)
- Transfer (Shift responsibility)
- Accept (Due to biz or cost constraints)

7. 4 key questions:
- What are we working on?
- What can go wrong?
- What are we going to do about it?
- Did we do a good enough job?
